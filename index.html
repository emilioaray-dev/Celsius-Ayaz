Dr. Humberto Fernández-Morán

The man who impact a billion lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Humberto Fernández life:

  • 1924 - Born in in Maracaibo, Venezuela
  • 1944 - He studied medicine at the University of Munich, where he graduated summa cum laude.
  • 1957 - Fernández-Morán was commissioned in 1957 with the supervision of the first Venezuelan research nuclear reactor, the RV-1 nuclear reactor, one of the first in Latin America.
  • He worked with NASA for the Apollo Project and taught in many universities, such as MIT, University of Chicago and the University of Stockholm.
  • 1967 - The John Scott Award, for his invention of the diamond scalpel.
